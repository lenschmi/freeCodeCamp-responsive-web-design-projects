Ueli Steck

One of the Greatest Alpinists of Our Time

Here is a time line of Ueli Steck's Life and His Greatest Achievements

  • 1976 - Ueli Steck was born on October 4th in Langnau im Emmental, Switzerland
  • 1993 - At age 17 Steck achieved the 9th difficulty rating in climbing.
  • 1994 - Steck climbed the North Face of the Eiger and the Bonatti Pillar on the Mt. Blanc massif, significant accomplishments for an 18 year old.
  • 2004 - With Stephan Sieggrist, Steck climbed the Eiger, MÃ¶nch, and Jungfrau within 25 hours.
  • 2005 - Steck was named one of the three best alpinists in Europe by Climb magazine for the Khumbu-Express Expedition, which included the first solo climbs of the north wall of Cholatse and the east wall of Taboche.
  • 2007 - Steck sets his first speed record on the North Face of the Eiger, completing the climb in 3 hours and 54 minutes.
  • 2008 - Steck bettered his own Eiger North Face record, reducing his time to 2 hours, 47 minutes, and 33 seconds. He also received the Eiger Award for his mountaineering achievements.
  • 2013 - Steck was involved in a confrontation with many Sherpas on the Mount Everest South Col route that became an international media event.
  • 2013 - Steck soloed the Lafaille route on the South Face of Annapurna in 28 hours. This earned him a Piolet d'Or.
  • 2014/2015 - With Michael Wohlleben, Steck linked up the three north faces of Tre Cime di Lavaredo/Drei Zinnen in 16 hours.
  • 2015 - In the summer of 2015 Steck climbed each of the 82 peaks in the Alps that are taller than 4000m. He did this over a 62 day period without using a motorized verhicle.
  • 2015 - Steck once again bettered his Eiger record with a time of 2 hours, 22 minutes, and 50 seconds.
  • 2017 - Steck died on April 30th on Nuptse while acclimatizing for a climb of the Hornbein route on the West Ride of Mt. Everest which was to be done without supplemental oxygen. Steck fell about 300m before reaching the summit of Nuptse; it is unknown what caused the fall.
